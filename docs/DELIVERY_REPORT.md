## 1. Executive Summary

**Chicken Road** is a polished, retro-arcade "cross the road" game that runs entirely
in a web browser — no installation, no account, no download. You guide a pixel-art
chicken forward through a scrolling world of grass, roads, rivers, and railways,
dodging cars and trains, riding logs across water, and grabbing corn for bonus
points — all while a camera that never stops rising keeps the pressure on. Fall too
far behind and an eagle swoops in to end the run.

The game is built in the tradition of *Crossy Road* / *Frogger*, with three distinct
themed stages of escalating difficulty: a bright **Countryside** by day, a neon-lit
**City** at night, and a grimy, hazard-lit **Industrial Zone**. Clear all three and
you win.

**Who it's for:** casual and arcade players on both desktop (keyboard) and mobile
(touch), and a portfolio/demo audience judging polish and "game feel." The whole
experience — every graphic and every sound — is generated by code inside one HTML
file, with nothing loaded from the internet while you play.

## 2. Feature List (everything delivered, in player terms)

### Character & animation
- **Hand-drawn pixel chicken** rendered crisp and chunky, the same character across
  all three stages (only the world re-skins).
- **Idle animation** on the title screen and at rest — the chicken bobs and pecks so
  the game feels alive before you even start.
- **Squash-and-stretch hop** — the chicken squashes on take-off, stretches at the top
  of its arc, and squashes again on landing, so every hop reads as springy and
  tactile.
- **Two death animations** — a feather-burst when hit by a vehicle or train, and a
  shrinking splash-sink when it falls into water.
- **Eagle swoop** — a larger bird-of-prey sprite dives in from the top, grabs the
  chicken, and carries it off-screen when you fall behind the camera.

### Movement & controls
- **One tap = one hop of exactly one tile** in any of four directions (forward, back,
  left, right) on a grid.
- **Instant, responsive control** with a small input buffer so rapid taps don't feel
  dropped.
- **Wall clamping** — the chicken can never hop out of the play column.
- **Obstacle blocking** — trees and rocks occupy tiles the chicken can't enter; a soft
  "bump" tone plays instead of moving.
- **Score only counts forward progress** — hopping backward, left, or right never adds
  to your score, and never subtracts from it either.

### Lane types & hazards
- **Grass / safe ground** — no hazards, but may contain impassable trees and rocks.
- **Road** — cars, trucks, and buses move horizontally at varying speeds and
  directions, each with a glowing headlight. Touching one is fatal.
- **River** — open water is lethal; **logs float across** as moving platforms. Stand on
  a log and it carries you along — but ride it off the edge of the screen and you drown.
- **Railway** — a **telegraphed warning** (flashing signal light + bell) fires before a
  **fast train** (5–25× faster than road traffic) rushes across the lane. Be off the
  track when it passes.

### The three stages (escalating difficulty)
- **Stage 1 — Countryside (Day):** bright, cheerful greens, rolling hills, gentle
  traffic, slow scroll, wide safe margins.
- **Stage 2 — City (Night):** cooler night palette, streetlights, a building skyline
  with flickering lit windows, faster and busier traffic including buses, medium scroll.
- **Stage 3 — Industrial Zone:** grimy browns and greys punctuated by red/amber hazard
  lighting, smokestacks and drifting embers, the densest and fastest traffic, the most
  frequent trains, no rivers, narrower safe lanes, and the fastest scroll.
- Each stage is cleared by reaching **row 30** of that stage.

### Scoring & corn
- **Score = furthest row reached** (carried forward across stages) plus a bonus for
  corn.
- **Corn pickups** spin on the lanes; collecting one adds **+3 points**, fires a
  sparkle burst, and pulses the corn counter in the HUD.
- **Session high score ("BEST")** is tracked and shown on the title and end screens,
  with a **"NEW BEST!"** badge when you beat it.

### Screens
- **Title screen** with the animated chicken, adaptive start prompt (both
  "PRESS SPACE TO START" and "TAP TO START"), and your session best.
- **In-game HUD** showing score, corn count, current stage, and a mute button.
- **Stage-clear screen** between stages showing score + corn.
- **Game-over screen** with a death-cause flavor line, final stats, and best.
- **Victory screen** after clearing Stage 3, with celebratory confetti.

### Audio (all synthesized — no sound files)
- Distinct sound effects for **hopping, collecting corn, the train warning bell,
  death, water splash, obstacle bump, stage clear, and game over**, all generated live
  in the browser via the Web Audio API.
- **Mute toggle** — click/tap the speaker button in the HUD, or press **M**.
- Audio automatically unlocks on your first interaction (to respect browser autoplay
  rules).

### Particles & "juice"
- **Dust puffs** on every hop (tinted to the lane you're on).
- **Feather burst** on impact death; **water splash** with an expanding ring on river
  death; **corn sparkle** on pickup.
- **Screen shake** on hard-impact deaths (car/train) and a **slow-motion white flash**
  on any death for punch.
- **Ambient particles** per stage (city window flicker, industrial embers, victory
  confetti).

### Mobile support
- **Tap to hop forward, swipe in any direction to steer.**
- The play area **scales to fit any screen** (letterboxed as needed) and stays crisp
  (pixel-perfect scaling), from small phones to large desktops — the same portrait
  play column everywhere, no layout reflow.

## 3. Screen-by-Screen Walkthrough

### Title screen
The Countryside scene sits behind the interface so the mood is set before you start.
The game title **CHICKEN ROAD** appears near the top, the **idle-animated chicken** bobs
as a hero element in the middle, and a blinking prompt below shows both
**"PRESS SPACE TO START"** and **"TAP TO START"** so keyboard and touch players are
both covered. If you've already set a score this session, a gold **"BEST: n"** line
appears. The mute button is available here too. Nothing plays sound until your first
input — by design.

### In-game HUD
A slim bar pinned to the top of the play area, laid out in three zones:
- **Left:** `SCORE` with the current value (furthest row + corn bonus).
- **Center:** a `STAGE n/3` badge that flashes on each stage transition.
- **Right:** the corn count in gold (which pulses when you pick one up) and the
  **mute button** (a speaker icon, showing a slash when muted).

Below the HUD is the live game world: the chicken, lanes, vehicles, logs, trains, corn,
obstacles, and the parallax background for the current stage.

### Stage-clear screen
When you reach row 30 of a stage, the scene freezes behind a dark scrim and a panel
announces **"STAGE n CLEAR!"** in green, listing your **SCORE** and **CORN** collected.
It advances automatically to the next stage — no button press needed.

### Game-over screen
On any death, the last frame freezes behind a red-tinted scrim and a panel shows
**"GAME OVER"** in red, followed by a one-line cause of death drawn from a fixed set:
**"SQUASHED!"** (vehicle), **"STRUCK BY TRAIN!"** (train), **"SWEPT AWAY!"** (water or
carried off a log), or **"SNATCHED BY AN EAGLE!"** (fell behind). It then shows
**SCORE**, **BEST**, and a **"NEW BEST!"** badge if you beat your session record, with a
blinking **"SPACE / TAP TO RESTART"** prompt.

### Victory screen
After clearing Stage 3, a celebratory panel shows **"VICTORY!"** in gold over a warm
gold scrim, with slow-drifting confetti, your final **SCORE** and **BEST** (plus
"NEW BEST!" if applicable), and a **"SPACE / TAP TO PLAY AGAIN"** prompt.

## 4. User Flow

1. **Title** → press Space or tap to begin.
2. **Stage 1 (Countryside)** → hop forward across grass, road, and river lanes,
   grabbing corn, until you reach row 30.
3. **Stage-clear** → score + corn shown → auto-advances.
4. **Stage 2 (City)** → same, harder and faster → row 30 → stage-clear.
5. **Stage 3 (Industrial)** → hardest, train-heavy → row 30.
6. **Victory** → "PLAY AGAIN" restarts a fresh run from Stage 1.

At **any point**, a death (vehicle, water, train, or falling behind to the eagle) ends
the run → **Game-over** → restart begins a **fresh single-life run at Stage 1 with score
reset to 0** (there are no checkpoints and no extra lives). The session high score
carries across restarts until the browser tab is closed or reloaded.

## 5. Tech & Architecture (in brief, accessible terms)

- **One self-contained file.** The entire game — code, graphics, and sound — lives in a
  single `index.html`. There are **no external images, fonts, audio files, libraries,
  frameworks, or network calls**; nothing is fetched from the internet while you play.
  This was verified by the QA team (zero network requests during load and play).
- **Drawn, not loaded.** Every sprite (chicken, vehicles, logs, train, eagle, corn,
  obstacles, scenery) and all text are drawn programmatically with the HTML5 Canvas 2D
  API, including a hand-built pixel bitmap font, so everything stays crisp at any scale.
- **Synthesized sound.** Every sound effect is generated live in the browser using the
  Web Audio API (oscillators), so there are no audio files to load.
- **Clean class structure.** The code is organized into clear building blocks —
  `Player`, `Lane`, `Vehicle`, `Log`, `Train`, `Eagle`, `Particle` / `ParticleSystem`,
  `Camera`, `AudioManager`, `InputManager`, and a central `Game` controller — all twelve
  confirmed present by QA.
- **Smooth, frame-rate-independent motion.** A ~60fps game loop (via
  `requestAnimationFrame`) uses **delta-time** movement, so vehicles, hops, trains, logs,
  and the camera all move at a consistent real-world speed whether the display runs at
  60Hz or 120Hz, with a guard against stutter when the browser tab is switched away.
- **Responsive by scaling.** The game renders at a fixed internal portrait resolution
  (336×576) and scales-to-fit the viewport with letterboxing, keeping pixels sharp and
  gameplay identical across phones and desktops.

## 6. Quality & Testing

The game went through **two full QA cycles** by the dedicated QA engineer, using a
Playwright-driven headless-Chromium harness plus targeted investigation scripts, all
against the shipped single file opened directly (no server, no build).

- **Cycle 1: 55 test cases** — 43 passed, **5 real defects found**, 2 failures traced to
  the test harness itself (not the product), 5 items flagged as needing a human (see
  below), 0 skipped.
- **Cycle 2 (re-verification): 47 test cases** — all **5 defects independently confirmed
  fixed** by two separate methods each (the developer's own regression script plus the
  tester's from-scratch repros with wider samples). **Zero new product defects.** Verdict:
  **PASS — ready to ship.**

### The 5 defects found and fixed
| ID | Issue (plain language) | Severity | Status |
|----|------------------------|----------|--------|
| BUG-1 | Score wasn't reset to 0 immediately on restart (stale value briefly shown) | Major | Fixed & verified |
| BUG-2 | Train warning bell rang on every frame (~87×) instead of once per flash — a buzzing sound | Major | Fixed & verified |
| BUG-3 | A developer/QA URL shortcut crashed if given a non-numeric stage value | Minor | Fixed & verified |
| BUG-4 | Buses appeared in Stage 3 (they should be Stage 2 only, per the design) | Minor | Fixed & verified |
| BUG-5 | A developer/QA "jump ahead" URL shortcut sometimes dropped the test player onto a hazard | Minor | Fixed & verified |

*(BUG-3 and BUG-5 both affect internal developer/QA URL shortcuts only, not anything a
normal player can reach.)*

### Acceptance-criteria coverage (AC-1 … AC-14)
All 14 acceptance criteria were exercised to the fullest extent automation allows:
- **Fully passing, no caveats:** AC-1 (boot & title), AC-3 (grass), AC-4 (road),
  AC-5 (river), AC-6 (railway — bell bug fixed), AC-8 (corn & scoring — score-reset bug
  fixed), AC-9 (stages & stage-clear — bus bug fixed), AC-10 (death/restart —
  restart bug fixed), AC-14 (code structure).
- **Mechanically verified, with a subjective/real-device portion that needs a human:**
  AC-2 (hop mechanics pass; "feel" needs a human), AC-7 (camera & eagle mechanics pass;
  "tension" is subjective), AC-11 (audio wiring, mute, and unlock pass; real-speaker
  audibility needs a human), AC-12 (delta-time independence verified in code; true
  sustained 60fps needs a real display), AC-13 (emulated swipe + responsive scaling pass;
  real-finger touch feel needs a physical device).

### Verified automatically vs. needs human playtest
- **Verified by automation:** no console errors, zero network requests, all 12 classes
  present, hop/score/restart logic, obstacle and wall blocking, corn pickup, stage
  progression and stage-clear at row 30, difficulty escalation, train timing, vehicle
  spawn rules, eagle fall-behind trigger, mute toggle, audio-context unlock, responsive
  scaling at phone and desktop viewports, and robustness under stress (reloads,
  tab-away, garbage input, extreme resizes).
- **Requires a human playtest (cannot be automated):** subjective **game feel** (hop

  real-device **touch feel/threshold**, and sustained **60fps on a physical display**.
  These were explicitly *not* passed silently — they are honestly listed as open in the

## 8. Known Limitations (honest list)

- **ISS-01 — Game feel not yet human-confirmed (the brief's #1 priority).** Hop
  crispness, camera tension, and death punch all match the design's timing values in
  code, but "does it *feel* great" is a subjective judgment a script cannot make. A human
  playtest pass is recommended to close this out. *Status: open (human-playtest only).*
- **ISS-02 — Audio audibility on real devices.** Sound wiring, mute, and browser
  autoplay-unlock are all verified in code, but perceived loudness and balance on real
  desktop/phone speakers need a human ear. *Status: open (human-playtest only).*
- **ISS-03 — Touch feel / swipe threshold on real devices.** Swipe and tap logic are
  verified under emulation, but real-finger latency and whether the swipe sensitivity
  feels right need a physical touchscreen. *Status: open (human-playtest only).*
- **BUG-6 — QA tooling only (not in the shipped product).** The internal Playwright test
  scripts lack a locked dependency file, so a clean-room reinstall of the *test harness*
  would need setup. This has **zero impact on the game** — `index.html` ships as a
  zero-dependency, zero-network single file. *Status: won't-fix (dev tooling only).*
- **Session-only high score.** Your best score is remembered only for the current browser
  session; reloading or closing the tab resets it. Cross-session saving was intentionally
  out of scope for this version.
- **No external assets, by design.** All art and audio are code-generated; there is
  deliberately no soundtrack/music and no image or font files.

*No Critical or Major defect ships open. The only Major-tagged open item, ISS-01, is by
nature a human-judgment item and is surfaced here for a playtest, not a code defect.*

## 9. Roadmap / Next Steps

1. **Game-feel tuning pass (highest priority).** Run a human playtest against ISS-01 and
   fine-tune hop timing, camera easing, and death punch based on real hands-on feedback.
2. **Persistent high score.** Add `localStorage` so a player's best survives reloads and
   return visits (small, self-contained addition).
3. **Real-device audio & touch tuning.** Close ISS-02 and ISS-03 with playtests on actual
   phones and speakers; adjust the swipe threshold and SFX levels if needed.
4. **More content.** Additional stages, lane layouts, vehicle/obstacle variety, or an
   optional endless mode past Stage 3.
5. **Optional sound & music.** An optional synthesized background track and richer SFX
   layers, plus a volume control alongside the existing mute.
